Methodology:

Steps to navigate through this visualization

  1. The first visual will be a chloropleth showing the deaths per state. Darker color implies more deaths.
  2. Click on any state to zoom in into that state and get more detailed visualization of that state.
  3. Above this section you can see the details about victims.
  4. You can hover over any element to get even more details.
  5. The bubbles on the map are colored blue and red to show male and female deaths(respectively) normalized by the population in that city/town/county.
  6. On the grid, the reds show female deaths and the blues show male deaths. The greys depicts unknown gender. The darkness of the colors are based on the age groups(Child/Adoloscent/Adult) of the victim. Darker colors correspond to higher age group.
  7. Double click on state to zoom out.
